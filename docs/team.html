01

The shift — from implementer to orchestrator

If the fleet does the implementing, what is left for the people? The question now has evidence. Every claim on this page went through the same adversarial verify pattern the fleet itself uses — independent skeptics, a vote, refuted claims excluded — as of July 2026.

Boris Cherny, the creator of Claude Code, describes the traditional functions — engineering, product, design, data science — “melting” into a single kind of role, and names five archetypes he observes on his own team:

Prototyper· Builder· Sweeper· Grower· Maintainer
The delegation gap

Anthropic’s internal study of its own engineers — the most agent-forward team measured — found AI in roughly 60% of all work, but only 0–20% of tasks fully delegable. Supervision, validation, and judgment stay human; the archetypes above are shapes of judgment, not of typing.

The review economics

Reviewing every generated artifact at human speed consumes the productivity gain — telemetry across 10,000+ developers shows review time up 91% and change size up 154%. Blanket human review does not survive contact with a fleet; oversight has to become tiered and escalation-based.

Both findings point the same way: the human seat moves from writing the change to owning what may pass. That is exactly why the fleet moves review into the machine — the survival vote — and reserves people for the ◆ gates.
02

What regulation locks in place

In a bank, three constraints on the team are not a preference. Each survived verification independently — and each lands on a different owner of the boundary.

Accountability cannot be delegated Human

  • UK SM&CR places accountability with named senior managers “regardless of automation degree” — the FCA’s line: “I did not understand it” is not a defence
  • so the ◆ gates — epic and contract ratification, money / PII sign-off — carry a named human signature, at every level of automation

Generation and approval must separate Model

  • the agent that drafts a change is structurally barred from approving its own deployment — a maker/checker rule practitioners inside a regulated US issuer reached independently
  • in the fleet: the lenses are independent of the drafter, the vote is counted in code, and approval is a distinct seat

The gate itself must be computed Harness

  • which changes get a human gate is decided by a deterministic classification — regulatory impact, customer proximity, reversibility, data sensitivity — not by the model’s mood
  • in the fleet: blast radius is computed from the derived catalog, and the computation — not an opinion — forces the ◆
Three groups that never met — an academic oversight framework, a bank-consortium governance body, and a regulated issuer’s research team — drew the same line: keep the orchestration deterministic, keep the human named, compute the gate. It is the boundary this site opened with, arrived at from three directions.
The regulatory weather is live. In April 2026 the US agencies revised the interagency model-risk guidance and explicitly excluded generative and agentic AI pending a request for information; the UK PRA keeps AI/ML within model-risk scope, though whether a coding harness counts as a “model” is interpretive. Which second-line validation seats are mandatory is jurisdiction-dependent and unsettled — the fleet designs for the stricter reading.
03

The team around one fleet

Mapping the traditional seats onto the archetypes, under the one rule. The composition is this project’s own synthesis — each input verified, the mapping proposed, the pilot the test.

from architect + tech lead · grows

Epic & contract planner ◆

  • authors the dependency DAG and contract design at the workspace layer; ratifies before work spreads to the repos
  • resolves escalations the bounded loop surfaces
  • the highest-leverage seat — blast radius concentrates here (the Builder archetype, holding the plan)
from senior engineer · transforms

Orchestrating engineer

  • shepherds several per-repo feature machines in parallel — context-switching displaces single-threaded deep work as the core skill
  • reviews what escalates, not everything: the machine does first-pass quality control; the human takes the novel and boundary cases
new seat · platform

Harness engineer ▣

  • owns the gates, hooks, workflows, and the conductor as tested production code
  • in a bank this seat is the control function — audit and replay infrastructure is its product (the Maintainer archetype, pointed at the harness)
from qa · transforms

Oracle designer ▣

  • stops executing tests and starts designing what “pass” means: adversarial scenarios, the testability floor, criterion→test traceability
  • the oracle’s execution is code — write-locked suites, captured coverage — so the human’s judgment goes where code can’t
from product owner · grows in authority

Intent owner ◆

  • owns the Jira plane — intent, business value, sign-off — and the ◆ ratifications that regulation makes human-owned by law, not preference
  • absorbs Prototyper work as drafting gets cheap: the person closest to intent can now show, not specify
from sre · transforms

Maintainer & replay operator

  • runs the estate the fleet ships into — reliability, security, efficiency at scale
  • plus a duty that is new: operating the record planes — the replay trail is the investigation input when an incident ships despite the gates
What shrinks. The mid-level implementer seat shrinks most — feature implementation is the band the agents absorb, and one orchestrating engineer now carries what a small implementing team used to. The rung does not vanish; it re-enters through different work — the ladder below.

Mandatory by regulation, whatever the org chart says:

a named accountable approver ◆· an approver independent of any generating agent ◆· second-line validation — jurisdiction-dependent
04

The ladder — and what only running it will teach

When juniors no longer write most of the code, the promotion currency changes: seniority becomes the blast radius you are trusted to approve. Judgment scope replaces code volume.

Where a junior starts

  • review literacy — reading the lens output, the vote record, the diff behind it
  • spec & acceptance authorship — the testability floor is a teacher: a criterion that isn’t decidable is refused
  • Sweeper work — simplification, unshipping, optimization: the archetype with the gentlest blast radius
  • escalation triage — the queue of what the machine could not settle is a curriculum of exactly the judgment the ladder rewards

Why this is a design input, not an accident

  • the fleet’s escalation queue and review record are its natural apprenticeship surface — every contested call is logged with its evidence and its outcome
  • a bank that staffs gates without growing gate-keepers ends with seniors and no successors; the pilot should measure junior seat-time at the record, not just approver load
A candid list — what did not survive verification. No production measurement from a bank’s agentic fleet survived the refute panel — the widely-cited “50% development-time reduction” claim failed 3–0, so this page carries no productivity number. The archetypes are a deliberately hedged observation (“what I think is five archetypes”), not an org chart — building job titles from them overreads the source. And whether Sweeper / spec / triage work is a sufficient apprenticeship for blast-radius judgment is an open question no confirmed source answers. These are things the pilot measures; we won’t invent them.
